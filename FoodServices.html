Food Donation Amidst Sri Lanka's Crisis

July 2022

A debt crisis is disrupting life across Sri Lanka, where food and fuel are suddenly either unavailable or exorbitantly priced. The Great Compassion Fund is pleased to have donated food to fifty families in the ancient city of Anuradhapura, Srilanka in July 2022. We want to thank Ven. Sirinanda for organizing the donation event and for all arrangements. Many many thanks go to the donors, Ven. Alice Nguyen and Mr. David Gonzalez from Los Angeles, USA. May all living beings be well and happy. May the Noble Triple Gem bless you all.

Food Distribution At Jorpati, Kathmandu

July 2022

The NIBS Great Compassion Fund provided packets of rice and lentil for the extremely destitute people living in Jorpati. We thank Lotsawa and our dharma friend Tika Sherpa for making arrangements for this charity program. We distributed those food packets to 15 extremely poor people living in the Jorpati area near Boudha, Kathmandu. They find it extremely difficult to make both their ends meet. Some are even homeless and have to sleep near the street. We thank Mrs. Vimala Sperber from the depth of our hearts for compassionately donating to this noble cause.

Food Distribution At Lumbini

July 2022

The Great Compassionate Fund distributed 30 sacks of 25 kg rice and groceries to the extremely poor people of Lumbini, Kapilavastu, and the surrounding slum areas during our 3-day visit to Lumbini (July 29-31, 2022). We extend our heartfelt thanks to all the donors, Indra Kafle and the social workers from Lumbini, for their overwhelming love and support for our philanthropic projects.

Food Distribution At Lumbini (2024)

Dec 17, 2024

The Great Compassionate Fund provided 30 sacks of rice and groceries to impoverished individuals in Lumbini, Kapilavastu, and nearby slum areas during a two-day visit (December 17–18, 2024). We sincerely thank all the donors, as well as Indra Kafle and Manju Parajuli Dhakal, a social worker from Lumbini, for their immense love and support toward our philanthropic initiatives.
